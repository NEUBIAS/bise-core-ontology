ICAN data hub

Exploring clinical, genomics, and imaging data to better understand intracranial aneurysms

ICAN1 is a national and multi-centric research program aimed at better understanding, through diagnostic and predictive approaches, the risk of formation and rupture of intracranial aneurysms (IA). Familial forms of IA suggest a genetic component, but to date, only few genetic studies have identified causally-related specific gene variants2. To address this issue, ICAN - coordinated by the Nantes University Hospital - is collecting large and comprehensive datasets covering clinical observations, imaging examinations (MRI, CT angiography) as well as exome sequencing. 3000 included patients are expected by the end of 2018. Involving geneticists and data scientists in multi-disciplinary approaches is a key component to mine these complex datasets but data access is often an issue itself due to security constraints and multiple administrative regimes.
